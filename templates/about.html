About Password Strength Analyzer

Understanding how we analyze and improve your password security

How It Works

Machine Learning Analysis

Our analyzer uses a machine learning model trained on the RockYou dataset - one of the largest collections of leaked passwords. This enables our system to recognize patterns that humans might miss and identify passwords that are similar to commonly used ones.

Entropy Calculation

Entropy measures the unpredictability or randomness of your password. A higher entropy means your password is more random and thus harder to guess. We calculate entropy based on:

  • The length of your password
  • The variety of character types used (lowercase, uppercase, numbers, symbols)
  • The presence of patterns or common sequences
  • The distribution of characters

Time-to-Crack Estimation

We estimate how long it would take for a modern attacker to crack your password using:

  • Brute force attacks (trying every possible combination)
  • Dictionary attacks (using common words and variations)
  • Modern hardware capabilities (assuming 10 billion guesses per second)

This estimation helps you understand the practical security of your password in real-world scenarios.

AI-Powered Suggestions

Our system uses advanced natural language processing to generate personalized suggestions for improving your password. The suggestions aim to:

  • Maintain some recognizable elements from your original password
  • Significantly increase security through strategic modifications
  • Keep the password somewhat memorable
  • Provide detailed reasoning for the suggested improvements

Privacy and Security

We take your privacy seriously:

  • All password analysis happens locally in your browser
  • We don't store your passwords
  • Passwords are only sent to our server for analysis and are not logged or saved
  • We use secure HTTPS connections to protect your data in transit

Password Security Best Practices

Do:

  • Use different passwords for different accounts
  • Make passwords at least 12 characters long
  • Use a mix of character types (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols)
  • Consider using a password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication when available
  • Change passwords regularly, especially for sensitive accounts

Don't:

  • Use personal information (birthdays, names, etc.)
  • Use common words or patterns (password123, qwerty)
  • Share your passwords with others
  • Write passwords down on paper or in unsecured digital files
  • Use the same password across multiple sites
  • Include sequential numbers or letters (123, abc)
